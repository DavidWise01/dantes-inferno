Dante Alighieri's **Divine Comedy** — *all three books* — and its terrible engine: **Hell
as a machine of perfect justice**, every soul sorted to the circle its sin has earned. A
Florentine poet, lost at the midpoint of life in a dark wood, is led down through the Nine
Circles by the shade of **Virgil**, up the Mount of Purgatory, and at last to Paradise by
**Beatrice**.

This roll *is* that machine: the damned by their circle, the guides who pass through, and
the frozen traitor at the dead center of the world. The darkest punishments are handled
here as **archetype, not detail.**

## The books

The lineage is all three canticles — one hundred cantos in all:

- **Inferno** — Hell, 34 cantos *(the spine of this roll)*
- **Purgatorio** — the Mount, 33 cantos
- **Paradiso** — the Heavens, 33 cantos

Written c. 1308–1320. Characters by Dante Alighieri, referenced as **inspiration only.**

## The roll — sorted, as Hell sorts

- ✦ **The Travelers** — Dante · Virgil · Beatrice
- ◐ **Circle I · Limbo** — Homer · Aristotle
- ❦ **Circle II · Lust** — Francesca da Rimini
- ☔ **Circle III · Gluttony** — Ciacco
- ⚖ **Circle IV · Greed** — the Hoarders & the Wasters
- ⚔ **Circle V · Wrath** — Filippo Argenti
- 🜂 **Circle VI · Heresy** — Farinata degli Uberti
- 🩸 **Circle VII · Violence** — Pier della Vigna · Brunetto Latini · the Minotaur
- 🕳 **Circle VIII · Fraud** — Ulysses · Pope Nicholas III · the Malebranche
- ❄ **Circle IX · Treachery** — Count Ugolino
- ◆ **Lucifer** — *set apart* — the frozen center of the world

The order is the descent itself: the Travelers enter, the circles fall away beneath them
one by one, and at the very bottom — set apart — **Lucifer**, frozen to the waist in the
ice, down whose body the travelers climb to come out, in the poem's last line, *"to see
again the stars."*
